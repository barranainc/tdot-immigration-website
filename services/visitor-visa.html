TRV vs. eTA

Not every traveller needs the same travel authorization. Depending on nationality and travel method, a person may need a temporary resident visa or only an electronic travel authorization. Clients who are eligible to travel do not always understand what evidence they still need to present to show the trip is genuine and temporary.

TDOT clarifies the difference early so the application strategy matches the actual travel requirement.

Super Visa for parents and grandparents

The Super Visa can be an excellent option for parents and grandparents who want to stay in Canada for extended periods while visiting family. It is often more practical than waiting for a sponsorship opportunity, but it still requires attention to insurance, invitation letters, and financial support evidence.

TDOT helps families understand whether the Super Visa fits the goal and how to build the file properly.

Financial requirements and ties

Visitor applications are often refused because the officer is not satisfied that the traveller has sufficient funds, a realistic itinerary, or enough ties outside Canada. Ties can be professional, financial, familial, or academic, but they need to be demonstrated clearly and credibly.

TDOT helps applicants decide what evidence actually helps the file rather than overwhelming the officer with irrelevant documents.

How TDOT prepares a strong application

A strong visitor file explains who is travelling, why they are travelling, how the trip will be funded, where they will stay, and why the person is expected to comply with temporary status. That narrative is particularly important for first-time travellers, applicants with family in Canada, and those with previous refusals.

TDOT focuses on those details because visitor refusals often become harder to fix when the original record was weak or inconsistent.

Why clients call TDOT

Visitor files are often refused for reasons that sound simple: travel purpose, finances, family ties, or return intent. TDOT helps applicants build the explanation before those concerns arise.

FAQ

Questions clients often ask

What is the difference between a TRV and an eTA?

A TRV is a visa document required for many foreign nationals, while an eTA is an electronic authorization used by certain visa-exempt travellers. The right requirement depends on nationality and travel circumstances.

Can family in Canada sponsor my visitor visa?

Invitation and support from family can help explain the trip, but it does not guarantee approval. The visitor still needs a credible temporary-entry case.

Is a Super Visa permanent residence?

No. The Super Visa is a temporary-entry option for parents and grandparents, although it may allow longer stays than a regular visitor visa.

Why are visitor visas refused so often?

Common issues include weak travel purpose, insufficient financial evidence, concerns about temporary intent, and limited ties outside Canada.

Can TDOT help after a refusal?

Yes. A reapplication should be guided by the refusal reasons and a stronger document strategy, not by simply submitting the same package again.

Need a stronger visitor or Super Visa application?

TDOT can assess travel purpose, supporting funds, family context, and refusal history before you apply again.
