Business visitor entry

Some business travel to Canada falls under the business visitor category rather than a work permit. The distinction matters because an applicant who is really coming to work cannot simply describe the trip as business development. Meetings, conferences, site visits, and contract discussions may be possible without a work permit, but the facts must fit the category carefully.

TDOT helps clients frame the purpose of travel accurately so border or visa concerns are reduced before departure.

Entrepreneur and investment-linked pathways

When clients say business visa, they may actually mean an entrepreneur or investor immigration strategy. Canada does not have a single catch-all business visa. Instead, applicants may need to consider federal founder routes, provincial entrepreneur streams, or a staged strategy that begins with temporary entry and develops into a longer-term immigration plan.

TDOT explains those distinctions clearly so business owners do not lose time pursuing the wrong category.

Intra-company transfers

Executives, managers, and specialized knowledge workers may be able to enter Canada through intra-company transfer rules where the legal and corporate requirements are met. The corporate relationship, the role abroad, and the proposed role in Canada all matter. A weak corporate record or poorly defined position can create major problems.

TDOT helps assess whether the facts support an ICT route and what evidence the file needs before it is submitted.

Start-Up Visa overview

For founders with an innovative business concept and support from a designated organization, the Start-Up Visa program may become part of a longer-term immigration strategy. This route requires more than ambition; it requires a business model, supporting organization engagement, and careful preparation.

TDOT helps clients understand where the SUV program fits and when a different route may be more realistic.

How TDOT Helps

TDOT screens the commercial objective, timelines, corporate structure, and immigration category before the application begins. That makes it easier to decide whether the client needs a visitor strategy, a work permit strategy, or a more ambitious immigration roadmap.

For entrepreneurs, that early legal framing can prevent serious errors in how the purpose of travel or the business intention is described to IRCC or at the border.

Why clients call TDOT

Business immigration works best when the legal category matches the commercial goal. TDOT helps separate short-term entry needs from long-term business immigration planning.

FAQ

Questions clients often ask

Is there one single business visa for Canada?

No. The term business visa is common, but the right route may be business visitor entry, a work permit, a provincial entrepreneur stream, or a founder-focused immigration option.

Can I attend meetings in Canada without a work permit?

Sometimes yes, if the facts fit the business visitor category. The details of the trip matter and should be reviewed carefully.

Does investment alone guarantee immigration?

No. Canada’s business immigration routes usually require more than capital. The legal program requirements and the business plan both matter.

Can TDOT help with entrepreneur planning even if I am not ready to apply?

Yes. Early strategic review can help you choose the right entry and immigration sequence before you commit resources.

What if I already have a Canadian corporation?

That may help in some cases, but ownership alone does not determine eligibility. The proposed activity and supporting evidence still need review.

Need a business immigration strategy that fits your real goal?

TDOT can help you distinguish business visitor entry, work authorization, and entrepreneur planning before you make the wrong move.
