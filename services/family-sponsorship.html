Who can be sponsored

Canadian citizens and eligible permanent residents may be able to sponsor a spouse, common-law partner, dependent child, and in some cases parents or grandparents. Other categories can exist in limited circumstances, but the core family class is structured and documentation-heavy. TDOT helps clients identify the correct category and evidence standard before the application starts.

Because each relationship type raises different questions, the supporting documents are not interchangeable. A spousal file, for example, will not be built the same way as a parent sponsorship or a dependent child application.

Spousal and common-law sponsorship

Spousal and common-law sponsorship cases often turn on credibility, cohabitation records, communication history, joint documents, and clear explanations of the relationship timeline. Even genuine couples can face problems if the file is thin, inconsistent, or poorly organized.

TDOT helps couples present the history of the relationship in a way that is factual, clear, and proportionate. This is particularly important where there are previous marriages, cultural distance, long periods apart, or prior refusals.

Parent & Grandparent Program

The Parent and Grandparent Program operates through intake rules that can change, so not every family can rely on it immediately. TDOT helps families understand whether they should wait for a PGP opportunity, prepare documents in advance, or consider a Super Visa as the more practical route in the meantime.

That strategic choice matters because many families lose time pursuing a route that is not currently open to them.

Super Visa as an alternative

The Super Visa can be a powerful option for parents and grandparents who want longer-term temporary stays in Canada without waiting on a sponsorship opportunity. However, it still requires careful financial and insurance planning. TDOT helps families compare temporary entry goals with long-term sponsorship planning.

Used properly, the Super Visa can support family unity while a broader permanent plan develops.

Dependent Children and sponsor eligibility

Sponsoring a dependent child may appear simpler, but proof of relationship, custody, consent, and age-related dependency rules can become central. The sponsor’s own status, undertakings, and financial position also matter across family-class applications.

TDOT reviews both sides of the file — sponsor and applicant — because sponsorship problems often arise from sponsor-side omissions rather than the dependent’s documents alone.

Processing timelines and how TDOT helps

Family-class timelines can vary depending on the category, the country involved, interview needs, and file complexity. TDOT sets expectations carefully and focuses on building a complete application that reduces the need for clarification later.

That means organizing relationship evidence, drafting explanations where needed, and helping clients understand what supporting proof is persuasive rather than redundant.

Why clients call TDOT

Family sponsorship is deeply personal, which is why documentation and timelines need calm, organized handling. TDOT builds files that are both legally complete and easy for officers to follow.

FAQ

Questions clients often ask

Can I sponsor my spouse if we live in different countries?

Yes, in many cases you can, but the application must clearly document the relationship and ongoing contact. TDOT helps present the timeline properly.

Can permanent residents sponsor family members?

Yes, eligible permanent residents can sponsor in several family-class categories, subject to legal requirements.

Is the Super Visa the same as sponsorship?

No. The Super Visa is a temporary-entry option for parents and grandparents, while sponsorship seeks permanent residence.

Do income rules matter?

They can, especially in certain categories. TDOT reviews sponsor eligibility and financial obligations before the file is submitted.

What if our relationship has unusual circumstances?

Unusual facts do not automatically mean refusal, but they often require stronger documentation and careful explanation.
